Shield DNS
How it Works

Privacy First.
Standard Everywhere.

Shield DNS is a private resolver that blocks ads, trackers, and malicious content before they even reach your device.

Why use a Private DNS?

🛡️ Ad Blocking

Stops intrusive ads and popups at the network level for a cleaner web experience.

👁️ Privacy

Prevents your ISP and trackers from seeing which websites you visit.

⚡ Speed

Faster lookups mean pages start loading instantly, with less background noise.

One-Time Browser Setup

The easiest way to use Shield DNS is to configure it directly in your browser. This works on Windows, Mac, iOS, and Android.

1

Copy Settings Path

Browsers block external links to their settings for security. Click below to copy the path to your settings:

2

Open Settings

Open a **new tab** and paste the path you just copied into your address bar.

3

Enable Secure DNS

Scroll down to "Use secure DNS". Choose "Custom" and paste your personal Shield DNS URL from your dashboard.

1

Copy Privacy Path

2

Configure DNS

Paste the path in a new tab. In the Security section, enable "Use secure DNS" and paste your Shield URL.

1

Copy Privacy Path

2

Enable Protection

Paste in a new tab. Scroll to Network Settings → Settings... and enable "DNS over HTTPS" with your custom URL.

Browsers like Chrome and Edge on Mobile support Secure DNS settings just like Desktop. Follow the same steps in the Mobile browser settings!

💡 Pro Tip for Mobile

For system-wide protection on Android, you can use the Private DNS setting, but it requires a hostname (without https://). For the best experience on all sites, use the browser-level setup above.
